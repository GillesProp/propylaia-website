A simple idea:
when we die, our story should not, and can not die with us.

MemoJo is a project to help each person leave behind a clear, honest and warm life story for the people they love — instead of a chaos of photos, accounts and files that nobody really knows how to use.

The same pain for everyone Sooner or later, every family goes through the same moment: the phone call, the shock, and then the question — “What do we have left of them?” Usually: a few souvenirs and a lot of digital mess.
From fragments to a real memoir MemoJo’s long-term goal is simple: turn a lifetime of scattered fragments into a single, meaningful memoir that feels like the person — and that stays for the next generations.
This 1-pager is not about the technical details. It is about the vision and why “the death moment” is the right starting point.

MemoJo - The 1,000 years vision
The problem today

We keep everything, but we lose the story.

A century ago, most people left behind a few photos and letters. Today, each of us leaves millions of digital traces: photos, messages, posts, videos, documents.

It looks like we are “better covered”, but in reality it is the opposite: it is almost impossible for a son, a daughter or a partner to rebuild a clear life story from this digital noise.

  • Too many files, not enough guidance.
  • Too much “social media persona”, not enough truth.
  • No single place where the story is told from the inside.
The wedge · Why start with death

The hardest moment is also the clearest need.

When someone dies, families are in pain and in need of answers. They do not want “more content”. They want:

  • A way to hear the person’s voice again.
  • A clear, honest story of who they were, in their own words.
  • Something they can hold in their hands and share over time.
MemoJo’s cornerstone: use this very specific moment — the loss of a loved one — as the entry point. It is where the need is the strongest and where a well-done memoir can make the biggest difference. From there, we can grow to broader use cases (life reflection, family history, local archives, etc.).

What MemoJo will do

In simple words: a guided path from life to memoir.

During life, MemoJo gently helps a person:

  • Choose their important memories (not everything, only what matters).
  • Add context and meaning — why this moment, what changed, who was involved.
  • Organise the story in chapters and phases of life.

At the end, MemoJo produces a private, recipient-ready memoir (digital and printed), which can be delivered to the people the user has chosen, after their death.

Long-term vision

From one family’s grief to a quiet, human library.

The first goal of MemoJo is very concrete: help families suffer a little less after a loss, by giving them a true, structured, heartfelt story of the person they loved.

Over time, as more people use MemoJo, it can become something larger: a discreet, private “library of lives”, where personal stories are preserved carefully for children, grandchildren and beyond.
